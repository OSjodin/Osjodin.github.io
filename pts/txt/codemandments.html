Once upon a time as a youngling, fresh out of my first webdesign class, my professor, a master in the art of surfing (the web), could smell my hubris from a mile away. He pulled me aside and laid down the law. Today I will share his sacred words.

1. Thou shalt keep it responsive:

Make sure your website looks fly on all devices, from big-ass desktop screens to tiny mobile screens. Responsiveness is the name of the game.

2. Thou shalt not go overboard with flash(y) graphics:

Yeah, we all love some eye candy, but too much of it can slow down your site and drive visitors insane. Keep it clean, sleek, and lightning fast. (A different time for sure. RIP Flash, may you never return)

3. Thou shalt embrace the power of white space:

don't be afraid of empty spaces. White space is like a breath of fresh air for your design. It gives your content room to breathe and makes it easier on the eyes.

4. Thou shalt slay the typography game:

Fonts are like tattoos for your website. Choose them wisely, make them legible, and keep them consistent. No one likes to squint to read your gnarly content.

5. Thou shalt make navigation a breeze:

Ain't nobody got time for confusing menus. Keep your navigation simple, intuitive, and in your visitor's face. They should be able to find what they need with no more than a few clicks.

6. Thou shalt optimize for speed:

Nobody likes waiting for a website to load. Optimize your images, minify your code, and do whatever it takes to make your site lightning fast.

7. Thou shalt be mobile-friendly:

Mobile is where it's at. More people browse the web on their phones than ever before. So, make sure your site is mobile-friendly, or you'll be missing out on some serious traffic.

8. Thou shalt be dynamic:

They say it's the inside that counts, and it couldn't be more true for web development. As beautiful your site is on the outside, so shall it be on the inside. Upon completion, a single tear of pure joy shall be shed, by how beautifully dynamic and pretty your code is.

9. Thou shalt test like no tomorrow:

Don't be lazy. Test your site on different browsers and devices. Fix those bugs, polish that UI, and make sure your visitors have a smooth ride, no matter which board they choose to surf the web on.

10. Thou shalt stay updated:

The web world is always evolving young padewan. Stay on top of the latest trends, tools, and technologies. Keep learning, keep growing, and keep pushing the boundaries of your web design skills.

There you have it. The 10 commandments of webdesign. As laid out to me by the man who for all intents and purposes, were our web-surfing Jesus.
